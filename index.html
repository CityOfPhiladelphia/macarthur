Reducing Philadelphia’s Jail Population: Share Your Feedback.

There are too many people in jail in Philadelphia, especially low income individuals and people of color. A significant number of those in jail are awaiting trial.

We believe that there are new policies and programs that could be put in place to reduce Philadelphia’s jail population over time, without risking public safety.

The City of Philadelphia is working together with the First Judicial District of Pennsylvania, the Philadelphia District Attorney’s Office, the Defender Association of Philadelphia, the Philadelphia Prison System, and the Philadelphia Police Department to develop a comprehensive plan to safely reduce the jail population.

Philadelphia’s plan is being developed as part of the MacArthur Foundation Safety and Justice Challenge. Philadelphia’s criminal justice agencies have been collaborating to develop a comprehensive plan, and are seeking grant funds from the MacArthur Foundation to implement that plan.


We want to hear from you:

What are the problems with the criminal justice system that need fixing? What do you think will help safely reduce Philadelphia’s jail population?
